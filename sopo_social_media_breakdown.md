# Sopo Social Media Content Strategy

This document details the visual theme, text copy, and scheduling strategy for all thirty stills in Sopo's social media campaign.

## Style System

We alternate three traditional Pacific patterns and four pastel colors to ensure no two posts look identical:

* Patterns:
  * Weaving: Horizontal coconut leaf lalanga weaving patterns (fifteen percent opacity)
  * Tapa: Vertical geometric ngatu tapa patterns (ten percent opacity)
  * Tatau: Subtle vertical Samoan tatau line motifs (ten percent opacity)

* Muted Pastel Colors:
  * Terracotta clay
  * Soft teal
  * Warm sand
  * Volcanic charcoal gray

## Posting Schedule and Strategy

We publish one video as a Reel and one still graphic as a Story every day from Monday to Friday. On the weekend, we repost the two best performing pieces from the week.

### Stills vs Videos

* Stills:
  * Purpose: Drive deep engagement and conversation. Still graphics are easy to read and digest.
  * Placement: Post as Stories daily from Monday to Friday.
  * Best for: Educational tips (MOFU) and clear calls to action (BOFU).

* Videos (Reels):
  * Purpose: Drive discovery and reach new audiences.
  * Placement: Publish to Reels daily from Monday to Friday.
  * Best for: Stories (TOFU) and debunking myths.

### Scheduling Strategy

* Daily Reels (5:30 PM to 7:30 PM):
  * Why: High traffic window when users are relaxing after school or work.
  * Frequency: Daily from Monday to Friday.

* Daily Stories (8:00 AM to 10:00 AM):
  * Why: High visibility during morning routines and coffee breaks.
  * Frequency: Daily from Monday to Friday.

* Weekend Reposts (Saturday and Sunday):
  * Why: Double down on successful content. Reposting the two best performing posts from the week maximizes weekend traffic.
